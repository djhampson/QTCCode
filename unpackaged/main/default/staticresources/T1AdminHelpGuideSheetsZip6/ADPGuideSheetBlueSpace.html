BlueSpace

Purpose
To determine and classify current product penetration by product line and geography in the ACCOUNT and identify the available headroom in order to identify future revenue opportunities and to provide a forecast for the three year horizon (current and the next two) of the Plan2Prosper.
Description
BlueSpace is the term given to a market space in which YOU have no business so far. The BlueSpace tool considers YOUR current penetration by product and geography, identifying the available headroom in to which YOU have the opportunity to grow. YOUR ACCOUNT penetration is classified as none (blue space), least, some and most, giving YOU a view of your share versus your two fiercest competitors.
  • The resulting Heat Map identifies patterns of current coverage, highlighting areas of future opportunity.
  • The Headroom tool allows YOU to view the ACCOUNT’s current spend (Estimated Realisable Value (ERV)), the competition’s share, YOUR share and YOUR forecast share for the next two years. A second view, Share of Wallet, allows a quick review of share by geography and product line.
Steps in the Process
  1. Determine the geography and business units for which YOU wish to conduct the BlueSpace analysis. (This is often pre-determined by YOUR business structure).
  2. For the prior year, record the total spend by the ACCOUNT on all products and services in the market in which YOU operate, (the ERV if YOU cannot address the whole of this market with YOUR current product set), the revenue that the ACCOUNT currently spends with YOU and the gross margin YOU make.
  3. Record the name of the top two competitors in the geographies for YOUR products. (This may be pre-determined by YOUR business) and record the revenue each currently has.
  4. Forecast the ERV by geography and business unit for the ACCOUNT and YOUR revenue for next year and the year after.
  5. View the resulting heat map for patterns in coverage and areas of opportunity.
  6. Review Headroom analysis, the ERV, competitor share and current and forecast business. Determine the validity of the forecast and amend as necessary.
  7. Record the fiscal objectives (and tasks) in Question 12.
Considerations
  • If patterns emerge within the Heatmap look for root causals of those patterns and set objectives and tasks to overcome these root causes.
  • Consider the extent of the challenge to YOUR account team in the forecast and set new objectives in light of the challenge.
