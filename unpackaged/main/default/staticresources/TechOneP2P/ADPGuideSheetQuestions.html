Purpose
To guide YOU to build a plan that establishes and delivers long term sustainable mutual business value with the resulting sales opportunities.
Description
The DSV Account Development Planning Process focuses on the three areas fundamental to achieving our account objectives.
    Three key questions in each of the three categories determine whether we:
  • Understand our customer’s business environment
  • Understand our business relationship with our customer
  • Have a plan to retain, make profitable and grow our business
Steps in the Process
  1. Document in the ‘Evidence’ box what you know concerning the criteria for answering the question and provide evidence that this is true. (Evidence should be in the form of triangulated opinions, witnessed behaviour or written comment)
  2. Evaluate whether the evidence supports that YOU have met the ‘Criteria to achieve green’ and record YOUR status as being ‘black (YOU do not know), red (YOU know that YOU have not yet satisfied the criteria) or green (YOU have satisfied the criteria).
  3. Having evaluated YOUR status, decide on the objective YOU wish to set regarding the specific question. If the status is black YOU are likely to set an objective to gain the information necessary to make a judgement. If it is red, YOUR objective will reflect the need to gain a positive outcome that helps YOU achieve green. If it is green YOU might think YOU have achieved all YOU need to achieve, or YOU might want to set an objective that makes sure the right people in YOUR ACCOUNT are aware of YOUR strengths in the specific area.
  4. Having set the objectives, YOU then set the activities (tasks and actions) that members of the virtual team need to do in order for YOU to achieve YOUR objective.
  5. On review, YOU must then decide if the activities have enabled YOU to meet the objective. If they have not then YOU will need to decide if YOU cannot reach a positive outcome or that YOU need to set new activities in order to achieve green.
  6. Once YOU have achieved YOUR objective, YOU then formally market it ‘closed’.
Considerations
  • Marking a question red – YOU know YOU have not satisfied the criteria is not bad. It tells you what you have to do to achieve your aims.
  • YOU may decide that YOU cannot reach a positive outcome. This does not necessarily preclude YOU from achieving YOUR goals, but it will mean YOU need to be confident that YOU can overcome this with YOUR strengths in other areas.
