Purpose
To guide you to build a winning sales plan (the Plan2Close) for your major opportunities.
Description
The 4x4 asks questions focus on the four areas fundamental to achieving a successful sale – qualification, justification, differentiation and influence.
  • Four key questions in each of the four categories (the 4x4) determine whether you have met the criteria for having discovered the opportunity as genuine so that you will not waste time, qualified from your own perspective that it is worth committing effort, time and resources, that you have a well constructed and differentiated value proposition and that you are targeting the right people to influence in your sales campaign.
  • Evidence is required that confirms you meet the criteria for answering each question positively.
  • On answering the question and depending on the answer you then set objectives and activities to reach those objectives so that you bring your Plan2Close alive.
Steps in the Process
  1. Document in the ‘Evidence’ box what you know concerning the criteria for answering the question and provide evidence that this is true. (Evidence should be in the form of triangulated opinions, witnessed behaviour or written comment).
  2. Evaluate whether the evidence supports that you have met the ‘Criteria to achieve green’ and record your status as being ‘black (you do not know), red (you know that you have not yet satisfied the criteria) or green (you have satisfied the criteria).
  3. Having evaluated your status decide on the objective you wish to set regarding the specific question. If the status is black you are likely to set an objective to gain the information necessary to make a judgement. If it is red, your objective will reflect the need to gain a positive outcome that helps you achieve green. If it is green you might think you have achieved all you need to achieve, or you might want to set an objective that makes sure the right people in the target prospect / customer are aware of your strengths in the specific area.
  4. Having set the objectives, you then set the activities (tasks and actions) that members of the virtual team need to do in order for you to achieve your objective.
  5. On review, you must then decide if the activities have enabled you to meet the objective. If they have not then you will need to decide if you cannot reach a positive outcome or that you need to set new activities in order to achieve green.
  6. Once you have achieved your objective, you then ‘close’ this off.
Considerations
You may decide that you cannot reach a positive outcome. This does not necessarily preclude you from the bid, but it will mean you need to be confident that you can overcome this with your strengths in other areas.
