9. Buying Criteria

Question
Are there formal buying criteria that the prospect / customer has articulated and do we understand them?
Objective
To establish the criteria by which the prospect / customer will judge suppliers on two levels;
  • Formal: RFP/RFQ – agendas/objectives of presentations, written specification etc for required solution and support systems, price, SLA standards.
  • Informal: Previous buying patterns, personal confidence, values, personal opinion of influential people, culture, values etc.
  • To determine the relative importance that the client puts on each of the criteria.
Criteria to achieve GREEN
  1. Discover both formal and informal buying criteria.
  2. Discover the relative importance the client puts on each of the criteria.
  3. Determine how we are perceived to perform against these criteria.
Examples
  • Formally: Reduce costs by $xxx; resilience(what do they mean by this?); SLA (looking for 2 hour response time); account management; looking for dedicated support team -(what does this mean?)
  • Informally: Assist individuals to achieve personal goals; trust (will you deliver what you say?); prospect / customer wants to be seen as a hero; they have deadlines to meet.
Considerations
  • Understanding of how our competition is perceived to perform.
  • Are they looking for a non-standard deal?
