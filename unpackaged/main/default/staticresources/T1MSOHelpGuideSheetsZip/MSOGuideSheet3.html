3. Budget / Procurement Process

Question
Has a formal purchasing process been initiated and a budget allocated? What are the key dates, evaluation and approval process, documentation required?
Objective
To determine whether a budget has been set aside for this project, that its value accommodates your possible solution and that you understand the purchasing process.
  • To identify who is involved in deciding which supplier they should work with.
  • To determine what practical steps are involved in the decision making process.
  • To determine what has to happen to move from one stage of the purchasing process to the next.
  • Uncover any evidence of previous purchasing commitments being vetoed unexpectedly and the reasons why.
Criteria to achieve GREEN
  1. Evidence that a budget has been allocated or is being considered for this project/initiative.
  2. Knowledge of the budget planning process and timing.
  3. Knowledge of the budget release process and sign-off procedure.
  4. Knowledge of the people involved in both of the above.
Examples
  • Budget set at $xxx or looking to reduce costs by 10% of a base of $xxx
  • Telecoms manager makes recommendations to IT director. Sign off by board
  • RFP issued, 2 weeks to respond, shortlist of 3 suppliers, suppliers invited to present within 1 week, decision made in 1 week subject to T’s and C’s
  • Who will actually sign the contract? Is that person available to sign the contract within the timeframe specified(e.g. not on vacation)
Considerations
  • At this stage do we anticipate any issues on pricing and our ability to achieve an acceptable margin?
  • Will this be a standard contract? If not, have you engaged the appropriate internal expertise to alert them?
