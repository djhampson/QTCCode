OrgChartPlus

Purpose
OrgChartPlus is the means by which to determine who and how to influence in the Plan2Close.
Description
OrgChartPlus is the means to determine who in the prospect / customer has what role in the decision making process, what their opinions of us are, their relationship with OUR virtual team and the dynamics that exist between them.
  • The Matrix enables you to plot contacts in the prospect / customer, and the members of your virtual team and understand the influence and opinion of your contacts and their relation with your virtual team.
  • The Decision Tree helps you map the formal decision making ‘organization’ determining the key players and mind mapping the various relationships.
  • The Political Web looks at you and your teams sphere of influence in the prospect / customer and helps you determine the politics of the sale.
Steps in the Process
  • In The Matrix, plot contacts in the prospect / customer and your virtual team.
  • Determine and record the influence the contact has on the bid, their opinion of you and the type of relationship they have with members of your virtual team.
  • In The Decision Tree, organize the contacts in to a formal hierarchy of who reports to whom in relation to the bid and determine who you need to meet as part of the presentation of your formal bid.
  • Mind map the relationships that exist between the contacts in order to capture the informal politics that will have a major influence on the bid. Determine who you need to influence in order to promote your offering.
  • In the Political Web establish who in your virtual team has the closest relationship and what the individual relationships are. Consider how you can use both the formal hierarchy and informal politics to deliver on your overall sales strategy.
  • Using OrgChartPlus record the relevant answers in questions 13, 14, 15 and 16.
Considerations
  • Remember the intent of the influencing section of the 4x4 – That is to determine that you have exposure at the right levels in the prospect / customer and that you know both the formal and informal environment that you are selling in to and finally, how you want to position yourself.
  • Also consider outside influences in the decision – namely external consultants or business partners.
Links
